# Day 3 Exercise – (lightweight) IMRAD Report

## Objective

Write a simple IMRAD report using Markdown; whist adhereing to the "less-is-more" principle.

- short factual statements.
- no extra unnescessary descriptions or areas discussed.
- to the point summaries.
- precise conclusion.

**NOTE:** in the final project, the IMRaD report will be the `README.md` file at the top level of the repository.

```md
# Title

**ABSTRACT** when the work is done, put your abstract here; we will define the abstract later in the course.

## Introduction

Summarises and connects societal impact, through challenges into your proposed solution and focus of inquiry with research questions.

## Methods

Describes the process implemented.

## Results

Presents findings, figures, plots and tables; only analysis, no synthesis - leave the latter to discussion.

## Discussion

Interpret results, reflect over method and future work; end with an conclusion.

## References

Listing 3-10 good and relevant references.
```

## Challenge

This challenge is a group work, using your group repositories to report the results.

- Add superscript or _(author, year)_ style references
- Start on an Introduction, identifying
  - an impact (society value)
  - a reference frame (theory), ie. scientific field
  - an challenge, ie. an open problem
  - a tentative solution (your work)
- Upload into your git **group repository**
  - add the document as the new `README.md` at the _top level_ of the repository,
  - this means that you should move your old `README.md` aside, ex. into `README_w1e2.md`

**NOTE:** the report does not have to be finished, but it should be present in its current state, within the group repository.
