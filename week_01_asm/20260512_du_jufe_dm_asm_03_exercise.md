# Day 3 Exercise – (lightweight) IMRAD Report

## Objective

Write a simple IMRAD report using Markdown; whist adhereing to the "less-is-more" principle.

- short factual statements.
- no extra unnescessary descriptions or areas discussed.
- to the point summaries.
- precise conclusion.

**NOTE:** in the final project, the IMRaD report will be the `README.md` file at the top level of the repository.

## Template

```md
# Title

Initially, this is a working title, centered on your intent. Along the road and by the end of the course, it will be a final title; with you results in mind.

## Introduction

Impact is described here at the very beginning, answering the question; why should your work be done, what does it do for society as a whole?
Problem description.^1

## Methods

Describe process.

### Method 1

### Method 2

## Results

Present findings. 1-3 figures, with plots and subplots; tables etc. are also acceptable. Short descriptive paragraphs on what the results are, no discussion - leave that to the next section. Figures must have captions, that stand on their own; meaning that a reader can read the figure caption, to understand the jist of it - without having to search for where it is referenced in the text. Every figure must be referenced in the text, otherwise, it's not part of the work and should not be in the report.

## Discussion

Interpret results, combining the findings with known facts from your reference frame, and motivate the value of the results in light of the impact - rendering a synthesis of your work in the conclusion. Other than that, a discussion includes

- Method reflection; discussing limitations of the chose method(s), and issues with its current implementation.
- Future reflection; answering, what is the logical next step - given the findings, and the results impact ie. your synthesis?

## References

5 to 10 references, of published, peer reviewed material. All other things borrowed, mentioned or built upon, will also need to be referenced. Less is more here too; any reference that is not mandated, because it is not used in your work, is not a valid reference. Chinese publications are good, but put titles, journals etc. translated to english in the reference section.

**NOTE:** expect that we during the course, will develop a common template for writing the reference section in this course. It will likely be a slimmed down version, of an already extant reference standard (probably Nature).
```

## Challenge

- Add superscript references
- Start on an Introduction, identifying
  - an impact (society value)
  - a reference frame (theory)
  - an challenge
  - a tentative solution (your work)
- Upload into your groups git repository

**NOTE:** the report does not have to be finished, but it should be present in its current state, within the group repository.
